# Crude-Oil-Market
Impact of the Crude Oil Market on the Economy Through Geopolitical Risk: Evidence from Russia and Germany.
Employing SVAR and Distributed Lag models, this project investigates the dynamic effect of geopolitical risk indices through oil structural shocks on real GDP, CPI, and exchange rates from January 1995 to December 2023 in Russia and Germany. The findings show that the relationship between geopolitical risk, oil prices, and macroeconomic aggregates changes over time. Oil price shocks exert a more substantial influence on the economies rather than geopolitical risks. The impact of the oil price shocks is greater in Russia than in Germany, proving that changes in oil prices trigger the economies in oil-exporting countries rather than oil-importing countries. The findings suggest investigating the effect of geopolitical risk on oil price changes as well as its effect on macroeconomic aggregates.
